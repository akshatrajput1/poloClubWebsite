ABOUT POLO

Polo is known as the Sport of Kings, a game where speed, strategy, and horsemanship unite on the field. In India, the sport flourished under the patronage of Rajput rulers, who celebrated polo as both a royal pastime and a test of warrior skill. Mastering the horse and mallet demanded precision, courage, and control, qualities that defined the Rajput spirit. Even today, the thunder of hooves and the swing of the mallet carry the same energy that once echoed through royal grounds. At our Polo Club, we honor this legacy while bringing the sport into the modern era, where tradition meets competition, and every match reflects the timeless elegance of polo.

MATCHES

India vs England – 1911 Coronation Cup Final

In 1911, the Indian polo team created history by defeating England in the prestigious Coronation Cup at Guards Polo Club. Representing the finest traditions of Rajput cavalry and horsemanship, players from royal houses such as Jaipur and Jodhpur showcased exceptional skill and strategy. This victory established India as a dominant force in international polo and marked a proud moment in the legacy of Indian royal polo.

India vs Argentina – 1957 World Polo Championship

During the mid-20th century, India continued its strong presence in international polo competitions. The 1957 era saw Indian teams competing at the highest level against powerhouse nations such as Argentina and England. Led by legendary figures like Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II of Jaipur, India demonstrated elite horsemanship, tactical brilliance, and a deep tradition of royal polo excellence.

India vs Pakistan – 2017 World Cup Playoffs

In 2017, the Indian polo team delivered a thrilling performance against Pakistan in the World Polo Championship qualifiers. The intense match highlighted the modern revival of Indian polo and the continuing spirit of competition in the sport. India’s victory reaffirmed the country’s growing presence in the global polo arena while honoring its centuries-old heritage of horsemanship.

PLAYERS

Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II (Jaipur)

Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II of Jaipur was one of the most celebrated polo players of the twentieth century. As captain of the legendary Jaipur Polo Team, he led his side to numerous victories in prestigious international tournaments. Born into the royal Kachwaha dynasty of Rajasthan, he developed exceptional riding skills from an early age. Under his leadership, Jaipur became one of the most powerful polo teams in the world during the 1930s. His elegant playing style, strategic brilliance, and dedication to the sport helped establish India’s global reputation in polo and made him one of the most respected figures in polo history.

Padmanabh Singh

Padmanabh Singh of Jaipur represents the modern generation of Indian polo players who continue the country’s royal polo legacy. Born into the historic Jaipur royal family, he grew up surrounded by polo culture and equestrian traditions. A skilled rider and competitive player, he has participated in prestigious international tournaments in England, Argentina, and other major polo circuits. Known for his speed, precision, and confident riding style, Padmanabh Singh has helped bring renewed attention to Indian polo on the global stage. His participation in international polo keeps alive the proud sporting heritage established by earlier royal polo legends.

Rao Raja Hanut Singh

Rao Raja Hanut Singh of Jodhpur is widely regarded as one of the greatest polo players India has ever produced. Born into the aristocratic family of Jodhpur, he inherited a strong tradition of cavalry riding and polo excellence. Hanut Singh was known for his exceptional tactical intelligence, calm leadership, and remarkable horsemanship. He competed at elite international polo tournaments and represented India with distinction. His ability to read the game and control the pace of play made him a respected figure worldwide. Even today, he is remembered as a master strategist whose influence shaped modern Indian polo and inspired generations of players.

Maharaja Sir Pratap Singh of Idar

Sir Pratap Singh of Idar was a distinguished Rajput nobleman, soldier, and renowned polo player who played a key role in promoting the sport during the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. A member of the Jodhpur royal family and regent of Idar, he was celebrated for his exceptional horsemanship and leadership on the polo field. Sir Pratap Singh helped popularize polo among Indian cavalry officers and royal courts, strengthening its status as a prestigious equestrian sport. His participation in polo and military campaigns reflected the deep connection between cavalry traditions and the game. Through his influence and patronage, he contributed significantly to the growth of polo in India.
